Frequently asked questions

How does the detection model work?

We use a hybrid model combining acoustic fingerprinting, spectrogram analysis, and vocoder artifact detection. This multi-layered approach allows us to spot common patterns in AI-generated voice, such as unnatural pitch, missing harmonics, or vocoder-specific noise.

What's the performance impact?

Minimal. The model is optimized for on-device inference and runs in a background thread, using hardware acceleration where available. Typical CPU usage is under 5% on modern devices during a call, with negligible impact on battery life.

Can it be integrated into our existing app?

Yes. Deepfake Guard is designed for integration. We provide a mobile SDK and APIs to embed the detection engine into your existing Android or iOS app. Our team can also assist with custom integrations for carrier-level deployments.

Is user data collected?

Privacy is a core design principle. By default, all audio processing happens on-device, and no call data or personally identifiable information leaves the user's phone. Enterprise clients can opt into a federated learning model for custom accuracy improvements without compromising end-user privacy.
